Is taking medicing bad?

Not all medicines are bad during pregnancy. Some are necessary for the health of the mother and baby, while others can pose risks, but, Some medications can harm fetal development, leading to complications or birth defects. It's essential to consult with a healthcare provider about which medcines are safe.

Why is it important to take medicine when pregnant?

Certain conditions, like high blood preessure or diabetes, may require medications to manage health risks. Ensuring the mother's health is crucial for the baby's well-being, as untreated conditions can lead to more significant issues.

Safe Medicines

Folic Acid

It helps your body make new cells(for example, blood cells, brain cells, skin hair, and nails). Every new cells in the body needs folate. Folic acid is especially important during early pregnancy, because it helps the neural tube-which becomes the brain and spine-develop.

Acetaminophen(Tylenol)

Acetaminophen is one the few pain medication that is generally considered safe to take during pregnancy. It is preffered to nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory(NSAID) drugs, such as ibuprofen, which are proven to pose pregnancy risks such as low amniotic fluid or fetal kidney problems.

Diphenhydramine(Benadryl) in moderate

Benadryl(Diphenhydramine) is an antihistamine that's considered safe during pregnancy, and is used to treat allergies, allergic reactions, sleep problems, and morning sickness. It's classified as a "Class B" drug during pregnancy by the FDA, meaning animal studies show it doesn't cause harm to a fetus.

Calcium carbonate(Tums)

TUMS provides safe heartburn relief for women who are pregnant. TUMS also adds calcium to your body. When you are pregnant, your body may need between 1,000mg and 1,300mg of elemental calcium per day. Be sure to take TUMS at a different time than you take iron supplements.
